INTERNAL MEDICINE QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE - Top 40 Conditions

1. ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME (ACS)
S/Sx: Chest pain/pressure, dyspnea, diaphoresis, nausea, radiation to jaw/arm
Tests: ECG (STEMI vs NSTEMI), troponin q3h×2, CBC, BMP, lipids, CXR
Calc: HEART score, TIMI risk score
Tx: MONA (morphine, O2, nitrates, aspirin 325mg), P2Y12 inhibitor, heparin, β-blocker, statin. STEMI→cath lab
Complic: Arrhythmias, cardiogenic shock, mechanical complications, reinfarction
2. HEART FAILURE EXACERBATION
S/Sx: Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND, edema, JVD, rales, S3 gallop
Tests: BNP/NT-proBNP, ECG, CXR, echo, BMP, troponin
Calc: NYHA class, EF%, fluid balance
Tx: Loop diuretics (IV furosemide), O2, restrict Na+/fluids, ACEi/ARB, β-blocker when stable. Consider inotropes if low output
Complic: Renal dysfunction, electrolyte abnormalities, arrhythmias
3. ATRIAL FIBRILLATION
S/Sx: Palpitations, dyspnea, chest discomfort, fatigue; irregularly irregular rhythm
Tests: ECG, TSH, BMP, Mg, troponin, echo, consider TEE pre-cardioversion
Calc: CHA2DS2-VASc (stroke risk), HAS-BLED (bleeding risk)
Tx: Rate control (β-blocker, CCB) vs rhythm control. Anticoagulation if CHA2DS2-VASc ≥2 (♂) or ≥3 (♀)
Complic: Stroke, heart failure, tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy
4. HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY
S/Sx: SBP >180, DBP >120 with end-organ damage (AMS, chest pain, dyspnea, vision changes)
Tests: ECG, troponin, BMP, UA, CXR, head CT if neuro symptoms
Calc: Target BP reduction (10-20% in 1st hr, avoid >25%)
Tx: IV labetalol, nicardipine, or nitroprusside. ICU monitoring
Complic: Stroke, MI, aortic dissection, renal failure, encephalopathy
5. COMMUNITY-ACQUIRED PNEUMONIA (CAP)
S/Sx: Fever, cough, dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, sputum production, rales
Tests: CXR, CBC, BMP, sputum/blood cultures, procalcitonin, consider legionella/pneumococcal urinary Ag
Calc: CURB-65, PSI/PORT score (admission decision)
Tx: Outpatient: amoxicillin or doxycycline. Inpatient: ceftriaxone + azithromycin. ICU: add vancomycin if MRSA risk
Complic: Sepsis, empyema, respiratory failure, parapneumonic effusion
6. COPD EXACERBATION
S/Sx: Increased dyspnea, cough, sputum production/purulence, wheezing
Tests: CXR, ABG, CBC, BMP, ECG, sputum culture if severe
Calc: A-a gradient, FiO2 requirements
Tx: Bronchodilators (albuterol, ipratropium), systemic steroids (prednisone 40mg×5d), antibiotics if purulent sputum. O2 to SpO2 88-92%
Complic: Respiratory failure, pneumonia, pneumothorax, arrhythmias
7. ASTHMA EXACERBATION
S/Sx: Dyspnea, wheezing, chest tightness, cough, accessory muscle use
Tests: Peak flow, O2 sat, CXR if severe, ABG if critically ill
Calc: Peak flow % predicted, FEV1 if available
Tx: Albuterol nebs q20min×3, ipratropium, systemic steroids, O2. Consider Mg, epinephrine, terbutaline if severe
Complic: Status asthmaticus, respiratory failure, pneumothorax, pneumomediastinum
8. PULMONARY EMBOLISM (PE)
S/Sx: Dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, tachypnea, hemoptysis, unilateral leg swelling
Tests: D-dimer (if low probability), CTPA, V/Q scan, ECG, troponin, BNP, lower extremity US
Calc: Wells score, PERC rule, PESI score
Tx: Anticoagulation (heparin, LMWH, or DOAC). Thrombolytics if massive PE with hemodynamic instability
Complic: RV failure, cardiac arrest, chronic thromboembolic pulmonary HTN
9. SEPSIS/SEPTIC SHOCK
S/Sx: Fever/hypothermia, tachycardia, tachypnea, AMS, hypotension
Tests: Blood cultures×2, CBC, CMP, lactate, procalcitonin, UA/urine culture, CXR, source identification
Calc: qSOFA, SOFA score, lactate clearance
Tx: Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1hr, IV fluids (30mL/kg crystalloid), vasopressors (norepinephrine) if MAP <65. Source control
Complic: ARDS, AKI, DIC, multi-organ failure, death
10. ACUTE KIDNEY INJURY (AKI)
S/Sx: Oliguria, fluid overload, edema, uremic symptoms, AMS
Tests: BMP (Cr), UA with micro, urine Na+/Cr/osmolality, renal US, consider renal biopsy
Calc: FENa, FEUrea, CrCl (Cockcroft-Gault), baseline Cr comparison
Tx: Treat underlying cause (prerenal: fluids; intrinsic: stop nephrotoxins; postrenal: relieve obstruction). Adjust meds for GFR
Complic: Hyperkalemia, metabolic acidosis, uremia, need for dialysis
11. HYPERKALEMIA
S/Sx: Often asymptomatic; weakness, paresthesias, palpitations, arrhythmias
Tests: BMP, ECG (peaked T waves, wide QRS, loss of P waves), repeat K+ to confirm
Calc: Transtubular K+ gradient if etiology unclear
Tx: Calcium gluconate (cardiac protection), insulin+D50, albuterol, furosemide, kayexalate, dialysis if severe/refractory
Complic: Cardiac arrhythmias, sudden cardiac death
12. HYPONATREMIA
S/Sx: Mild: nausea, confusion. Severe: seizures, coma, AMS
Tests: BMP, serum/urine osmolality, urine Na+, assess volume status, TSH, cortisol
Calc: Corrected Na+ (if hyperglycemic), Na+ deficit, rate of correction (max 8-10mEq/L/24hr)
Tx: Depends on volume status and acuity. Hypovolemic: NS. Euvolemic: fluid restriction, treat SIADH. Hypervolemic: fluid restriction, diuretics
Complic: Osmotic demyelination syndrome (if corrected too rapidly), cerebral edema
13. DIABETIC KETOACIDOSIS (DKA)
S/Sx: Polyuria, polydipsia, N/V, abdominal pain, AMS, Kussmaul respirations, fruity breath
Tests: BMP, glucose, VBG/ABG, β-hydroxybutyrate, UA (ketones), anion gap
Calc: Anion gap, corrected Na+, osmolality, fluid deficit
Tx: IV fluids (NS initially), insulin gtt (0.1 U/kg/hr after bolus), K+ repletion, treat precipitant. Add D5 when glucose <200
Complic: Cerebral edema (esp. children), hypokalemia, hypoglycemia, ARDS
14. HYPEROSMOLAR HYPERGLYCEMIC STATE (HHS)
S/Sx: AMS, severe dehydration, glucose >600, minimal/no ketones
Tests: BMP, glucose, serum osmolality, VBG, UA
Calc: Serum osmolality (>320), corrected Na+, free water deficit
Tx: Aggressive IV fluids (NS then 0.45% NS), lower-dose insulin, K+ repletion, slower correction than DKA
Complic: Thrombosis, cerebral edema, seizures, vascular events
15. UPPER GI BLEED
S/Sx: Hematemesis, melena, abdominal pain, hemodynamic instability
Tests: CBC, BMP, coags (PT/PTT), type & cross, LFTs. EGD for diagnosis/treatment
Calc: Glasgow-Blatchford score, Rockall score (pre/post-endoscopy)
Tx: Resuscitate (IVF, pRBC if Hgb <7), NPO, PPI IV (pantoprazole 80mg bolus→8mg/hr), octreotide if varices suspected. EGD within 24hr
Complic: Hypovolemic shock, aspiration, rebleeding, need for surgery
16. CIRRHOSIS WITH ASCITES
S/Sx: Abdominal distention, shifting dullness, fluid wave, peripheral edema
Tests: Diagnostic paracentesis (cell count, alb, culture, cytology), LFTs, CBC, PT/INR, abdominal US
Calc: SAAG (serum-ascites albumin gradient), MELD score, Child-Pugh score
Tx: Na+ restriction, diuretics (spironolactone + furosemide 100:40 ratio), large-volume paracentesis with albumin if tense
Complic: SBP, hepatorenal syndrome, hyponatremia, hepatic hydrothorax
17. SPONTANEOUS BACTERIAL PERITONITIS (SBP)
S/Sx: Fever, abdominal pain/tenderness, AMS, rebound/guarding (often subtle in cirrhosis)
Tests: Diagnostic paracentesis (PMN >250/μL), ascitic fluid culture (bedside inoculation)
Calc: Ascitic PMN count
Tx: Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 5d or cefotaxime. Albumin 1.5g/kg day 1, 1g/kg day 3. Prophylaxis: norfloxacin after episode
Complic: Hepatorenal syndrome, septic shock, death
18. HEPATIC ENCEPHALOPATHY
S/Sx: AMS, asterixis, confusion, personality changes, hyperreflexia
Tests: Ammonia (not always necessary), LFTs, CBC, BMP, identify precipitant (infection, GI bleed, meds)
Calc: West Haven criteria (grade 1-4), MELD score
Tx: Lactulose (titrate to 2-3 BMs/day), rifaximin. Treat precipitant. Avoid sedatives
Complic: Aspiration, cerebral edema (acute liver failure), progression to coma
19. ACUTE PANCREATITIS
S/Sx: Epigastric pain radiating to back, N/V, fever, tachycardia
Tests: Lipase (>3× ULN), LFTs, CBC, BMP, Ca, TG, RUQ US, CT if severe/unclear dx
Calc: Ranson criteria, BISAP score, APACHE II, fluid sequestration
Tx: Aggressive IV hydration (LR preferred), pain control, NPO initially then advance diet as tolerated. ERCP if biliary obstruction
Complic: Necrosis, pseudocyst, ARDS, AKI, shock
20. CELLULITIS
S/Sx: Erythema, warmth, edema, tenderness, fever, lymphangitis
Tests: Clinical diagnosis. Blood cultures if systemic symptoms. Consider US to r/o abscess
Calc: Mark borders to track progression/improvement
Tx: Cephalexin or cefazolin (IV if severe). Add MRSA coverage (vancomycin, linezolid) if purulent/systemic. Elevation, warm compresses
Complic: Abscess formation, necrotizing fasciitis, sepsis, osteomyelitis
21. URINARY TRACT INFECTION (UTI)
S/Sx: Dysuria, frequency, urgency, suprapubic pain, hematuria. Pyelonephritis: fever, CVA tenderness
Tests: UA with micro, urine culture. Blood cultures if pyelonephritis/urosepsis
Calc: N/A
Tx: Cystitis: nitrofurantoin, TMP-SMX, or fluoroquinolone. Pyelonephritis: fluoroquinolone or ceftriaxone. Tailor to culture
Complic: Pyelonephritis, urosepsis, renal abscess
22. C. DIFFICILE INFECTION
S/Sx: Diarrhea (≥3 unformed stools/24hr), abdominal pain, fever, leukocytosis
Tests: Stool PCR or GDH + toxin assay, CBC, BMP, lactate, CT if severe (toxic megacolon)
Calc: WBC count, Cr, albumin (severity markers)
Tx: PO vancomycin 125mg QID or fidaxomicin. If severe: vanc + IV metronidazole. Fulminant: vanc + metro + consider surgery
Complic: Toxic megacolon, perforation, sepsis, recurrence
23. DEEP VEIN THROMBOSIS (DVT)
S/Sx: Unilateral leg pain, swelling, warmth, Homan's sign (low sensitivity)
Tests: Compression US with Doppler (duplex), D-dimer (if low probability), consider thrombophilia workup
Calc: Wells score for DVT
Tx: Anticoagulation: LMWH, fondaparinux, or DOAC. Rivaroxaban or apixaban can start immediately. Duration: 3mo minimum
Complic: PE, post-thrombotic syndrome, phlegmasia cerulea dolens
24. ACUTE ISCHEMIC STROKE
S/Sx: Sudden focal neuro deficit: weakness, numbness, speech difficulty, vision loss, ataxia
Tests: Non-contrast head CT (r/o hemorrhage), MRI/DWI, CTA/MRA, ECG, lipids, HbA1c, echo
Calc: NIHSS score, last known well time
Tx: tPA if <4.5hr and no contraindications. Thrombectomy if <24hr and large vessel occlusion. ASA 325mg after 24hr. Admit stroke unit
Complic: Hemorrhagic transformation, cerebral edema, recurrent stroke
25. SEIZURE
S/Sx: Convulsions, LOC, post-ictal confusion, tongue biting, incontinence, focal deficits
Tests: BMP (Na, glucose, Ca, Mg), AED levels if on therapy, toxicology screen, head CT, LP if infection suspected, EEG
Calc: Corrected Ca, anion gap
Tx: Status epilepticus: benzodiazepines, then levetiracetam, fosphenytoin, or valproate. Identify/treat cause. Restart home AEDs
Complic: Status epilepticus, aspiration, trauma, SUDEP
26. BACTERIAL MENINGITIS
S/Sx: Fever, headache, neck stiffness, photophobia, AMS, Kernig/Brudzinski signs
Tests: Blood cultures×2, LP (opening pressure, cell count/diff, glucose, protein, Gram stain, culture), head CT before LP if ↑ICP concerns
Calc: CSF:serum glucose ratio
Tx: Empiric: vancomycin + ceftriaxone (± ampicillin if >50yo). Dexamethasone if bacterial. Don't delay antibiotics for LP
Complic: Brain abscess, hydrocephalus, seizures, hearing loss, death
27. ANEMIA
S/Sx: Fatigue, pallor, dyspnea, tachycardia, dizziness, angina if severe
Tests: CBC with diff, reticulocyte count, iron studies, B12, folate, peripheral smear, hemolysis labs if indicated
Calc: MCV (microcytic <80, normocytic 80-100, macrocytic >100), corrected retic count
Tx: Depends on cause. Iron deficiency: PO/IV iron. B12 def: B12 repletion. Transfuse if symptomatic or Hgb <7
Complic: High-output heart failure, MI, hypotension
28. NEUTROPENIC FEVER
S/Sx: Fever ≥38.3°C or ≥38°C for 1hr with ANC <500 or <1000 expected to ↓
Tests: Blood cultures×2, CBC, CMP, CXR, UA/urine culture. Consider imaging based on symptoms
Calc: ANC (absolute neutrophil count), MASCC score (risk stratification)
Tx: Broad-spectrum anti-pseudomonal β-lactam (cefepime, pip-tazo, meropenem). Add vancomycin if line infection, skin infection, or hemodynamically unstable
Complic: Septic shock, multi-organ failure, death
29. ALCOHOL WITHDRAWAL
S/Sx: Tremor, anxiety, N/V, insomnia, tachycardia, HTN, diaphoresis. Severe: hallucinations, seizures, DTs
Tests: BMP, Mg, Phos, LFTs, CBC, BAL, toxicology screen
Calc: CIWA-Ar score (symptom-triggered therapy)
Tx: Benzodiazepines (diazepam or lorazepam), symptom-triggered or fixed schedule. Thiamine, folate, multivitamin. IV fluids, electrolyte repletion
Complic: Delirium tremens, Wernicke-Korsakoff, seizures, aspiration
30. DELIRIUM
S/Sx: Acute onset AMS, fluctuating course, inattention, disorganized thinking. Hypo/hyperactive
Tests: Evaluate for underlying cause: CBC, CMP, UA/culture, CXR, medication review, head CT if indicated
Calc: CAM (Confusion Assessment Method)
Tx: Identify/treat cause. Non-pharm: reorientation, sleep hygiene, minimize restraints. Pharm: haloperidol or quetiapine if severe agitation
Complic: Falls, prolonged hospitalization, functional decline, increased mortality
31. HYPERTHYROIDISM/THYROID STORM
S/Sx: Weight loss, tremor, palpitations, heat intolerance, diarrhea, anxiety. Storm: fever, tachycardia, AMS
Tests: TSH (low), free T4 (high), free T3, TPO antibodies, radioactive iodine uptake
Calc: Burch-Wartofsky score (thyroid storm)
Tx: β-blocker, thionamide (PTU or methimazole), iodine (1hr after thionamide), steroids if storm. Supportive care
Complic: Atrial fibrillation, heart failure, thyroid storm, osteoporosis
32. HYPOTHYROIDISM/MYXEDEMA COMA
S/Sx: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, bradycardia, dry skin. Coma: hypothermia, hypotension, AMS
Tests: TSH (high), free T4 (low), TPO antibodies, lipids, CBC, CMP
Calc: N/A
Tx: Levothyroxine replacement. Myxedema coma: IV T4 + T3, hydrocortisone, supportive care, ICU admission
Complic: Myxedema coma, pericardial effusion, hyperlipidemia, infertility
33. ADRENAL INSUFFICIENCY/CRISIS
S/Sx: Fatigue, weakness, N/V, abdominal pain, hypotension, hyperpigmentation (primary). Crisis: shock, AMS
Tests: AM cortisol, ACTH, ACTH stim test (cosyntropin), BMP (hyponatremia, hyperkalemia), glucose
Calc: N/A
Tx: Acute crisis: hydrocortisone 100mg IV q8h, IV NS resuscitation, treat precipitant. Chronic: hydrocortisone + fludrocortisone (if primary)
Complic: Adrenal crisis, cardiovascular collapse, death
34. HYPERCALCEMIA
S/Sx: "Stones, bones, abdominal groans, psychiatric overtones" - kidney stones, bone pain, constipation, AMS, weakness
Tests: BMP, ionized Ca, PTH, PTHrP, vitamin D, phosphate, albumin, ECG
Calc: Corrected Ca (add 0.8 for each 1g/dL alb below 4)
Tx: IV NS hydration, calcitonin (rapid effect), bisphosphonates (zoledronic acid), treat underlying cause. Dialysis if severe
Complic: Nephrocalcinosis, AKI, arrhythmias, pancreatitis
35. HYPOMAGNESEMIA
S/Sx: Weakness, tremor, tetany, Chvostek/Trousseau signs, arrhythmias, seizures
Tests: Serum Mg, BMP (often concurrent hypocalcemia, hypokalemia), ECG
Calc: N/A
Tx: PO magnesium oxide or IV magnesium sulfate (1-2g over 15min if severe). Check and replete Ca and K
Complic: Refractory hypokalemia/hypocalcemia, torsades de pointes, seizures
36. RHABDOMYOLYSIS
S/Sx: Muscle pain, weakness, dark urine (tea/cola-colored), myoglobinuria
Tests: CK (>5× ULN or >1000), BMP (hyperkalemia, ↑Cr), UA (myoglobin without RBCs), phosphate, Ca, uric acid
Calc: CK level, urine output goal >200mL/hr
Tx: Aggressive IV hydration (NS 200-300mL/hr initially), monitor electrolytes (K, Ca, Phos). Stop offending agents. Alkalinization controversial
Complic: AKI (from myoglobin), hyperkalemia, DIC, compartment syndrome
37. TRANSFUSION REACTION
S/Sx: Fever, chills, pruritis, urticaria, dyspnea, chest/back pain, hypotension (acute hemolytic), TRALI
Tests: CBC, blood bank notification, direct Coombs, LDH, haptoglobin, bilirubin, UA (hemoglobinuria), blood cultures
Calc: N/A
Tx: STOP transfusion. IV fluids, supportive care. Febrile non-hemolytic: acetaminophen. Allergic: antihistamines ± steroids. Anaphylaxis: epinephrine
Complic: AKI, DIC, TRALI, TACO, death (acute hemolytic)
38. PLEURAL EFFUSION
S/Sx: Dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, cough, decreased breath sounds, dullness to percussion
Tests: CXR (>200mL visible), thoracentesis (fluid analysis: LDH, protein, pH, glucose, cell count, cytology, culture), pleural US
Calc: Light's criteria (exudate vs transudate)
Tx: Treat underlying cause. Therapeutic thoracentesis if symptomatic. Chest tube if empyema/hemothorax
Complic: Empyema, re-expansion pulmonary edema, pneumothorax (post-procedure)
39. PNEUMOTHORAX
S/Sx: Sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, decreased breath sounds, hyperresonance. Tension: hypotension, JVD, tracheal deviation
Tests: CXR (upright), CT if unclear. ABG if respiratory distress
Calc: Size (cm at hilum level)
Tx: Small/asymptomatic: observation + O2. Large/symptomatic: needle decompression (tension) or chest tube. Spontaneous: consider pleurodesis
Complic: Tension pneumothorax, recurrence, respiratory failure
40. SYNCOPE
S/Sx: Transient LOC with spontaneous recovery, prodrome (lightheadedness, diaphoresis), lack of focal neuro findings
Tests: ECG (all patients), orthostatic VS, CBC, BMP, glucose, troponin. Consider echo, Holter, tilt-table, EP study based on risk
Calc: San Francisco Syncope Rule, ROSE score (risk stratification)
Tx: Depends on etiology: volume repletion, medication adjustment, pacemaker if high-degree AV block. Admit if high-risk features
Complic: Trauma from fall, sudden cardiac death if cardiac etiology
