TIERED DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH
TIER 1: Acuity/Severity Assessment
TIER 2: Renal Function
TIER 3: Contributing Factors
TIER 4: Pathophysiologic Category
Decreased Excretion
#35 Acute Kidney Injury - Elevated Cr, oliguria
#34 CKD/ESRD - Baseline elevated Cr, anemia
Medication-Induced - ACEi, ARB, K-sparing diuretics
Type IV RTA - Low aldosterone, mild hyperchloremic acidosis

#47 DKA - High glucose, anion gap, total body K depleted
#41 Tumor Lysis Syndrome - High uric acid, phos, LDH, low Ca

‚ö†Ô∏è MUST NOT MISS
  • ECG changes - Peaked T waves ‚Üí Membrane stabilization with Ca gluconate

  • #41 Tumor Lysis - Allopurinol, aggressive hydration, monitor Q6h
üî¨ INITIAL WORKUP
  • ECG immediately - Peaked T, wide QRS, sine wave

  • ABG/VBG - pH, assess acidosis
  • Medication review - ACEi, ARB, spironolactone, NSAIDs
  • If suspected tumor lysis: Uric acid, phosphorus, calcium, LDH
  • If suspected rhabdo: CK, urinalysis for myoglobin
